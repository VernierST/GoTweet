Troubleshooting

Go!Tweet makes it easy to connect your LabQuest, LabQuest Mini, or Go! device and send its data to Twitter. Follow these tips in case Go!Tweet has trouble finding your interface.

  1. Try searching again for devices. Go to File > Search for Devices
  2. Make sure your device is powered on. When on, the LabQuest's power button will have a blue ring around it. On a LabQuest mini, Go!Motion, Go!Temp, and Go!Link a red, amber, or green LED will indicate if it is on.
  3. Check if the interface's USB cord is properly connected to the computer on one end and your interface on the other. Windows should notify you when your device is connected or disconnected from your computer.
  4. Make sure the appropriate drivers are installed and working properly for your device. If you have Logger Lite, Logger Pro, or the LabQuest Emulator installed on your computer, you should already have the necessary drivers. Otherwise, you can download the drivers as a stand-alone package at http://www.vernier.com/drivers. Make sure to restart Go!Tweet after installing the drivers. If Go!Tweet still does not work, try reinstalling the drivers because a bad installation can cause the driver not to work.
  5. Go!Tweet may not be able to find your Go! device if Logger Pro or Logger Lite are running. Please close them and then search for devices.
Note: Go!Tweet will connect to the first interface it finds. Go!Tweet does not work with multiple interfaces.
