Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Twitter

Q: What is Twitter?
A: According to Twitter, it is "a real-time information network powered by people all around the world that lets you share and discover what’s happening now."

Q: What is a tweet?
A: A tweet is a single message to Twitter that anyone with permission can see.

Q: Can I use Go!Tweet without a Twitter account?
A: You will need a Twitter account to post sensor data. To sign up, go to http://www.twitter.com/signup then follow the instructions on Connecting To Twitter

Go!Tweet

Q: What is Go!Tweet?
A: Go!Tweet is an easy way for anyone to quickly send Vernier sensor data to the internet through Twitter.

Q: What devices are supported in Go!Tweet?
A: Go!Tweets supports LabQuest, LabQuest Mini, and Go!Temp, Go!Motion, and the Go!Link.

Q: What Vernier sensors are supported by Go!Tweet?
A: Go!Tweet supports most sensors that can be plugged into an analog channel. For a complete list of sensors known not to work with Go!Tweet, please see the list of unsupported sensors.
 
Q: Why does Go!Tweet not recognize my interface?
A: Follow the troubleshooting guide for help on connecting your interface to Go!Tweet

Q: Why does Go!Tweet tell me that I have already tweeted this value recently?
A: In order to prevent spam, Twitter has measures in place to prevent duplicate updates. Unfortunately, there is no way we can go around this limitation currently.
